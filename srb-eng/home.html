SLAVKOVICA MONASTERY

In Ikonići, the southwestern hamlet of Slavkovice pod Rajcem, on the stretch of Manastirina are the remains of a monastery with a church from the end of the 13th or from the first decades of the 14th century. Long abandoned, the church is only, according to legend, associated with popular feast of the Assumption. The oldest and most important part of this whole is a single-nave temple, divided into three transepts by pilasters and completed irregular, horseshoe-shaped and extremely spacious altar apse. It is built skillfully, with unworked stone, with creepers as a façade decoration, while painted decoration seems to have never been. In the middle of the 15th century, next to the western façade of the church, a narthex was added, in which powerful stone stones were buried. sarcophagi, buried three eminent personalities, certainly the founders. The remains of two people were found in them men (older and younger) and one (older) woman, in whose hands a cameo with the image was found Saint Nicholas, who, based on some sources, is associated with the person of Jerina Branković, which is why some scientists believe that the graves in the church belong to her, her husband, despot Đurđe Branković and their son Lazarus. Precious is the finding of a cameo-icon with the image of St. Nicholas and the cross. It dates back to the X / XI century and is preserved today at the National Museum in Belgrade.
